# [10 All-time Great Articles chosen by Longformarticles.net](https://href.li/?https://longformarticles.net/)

![image](https://64.media.tumblr.com/637c64bcd451538e5aef442d474fd7a3/22bd9eea3e4db31a-24/s500x750/e08c784a6d5a4c667ed3405d8956498b4a6d7648.jpg)

If you’re looking for a daily dose of longform, check out [Longformarticles.net](https://href.li/?https://longformarticles.net/), a great new site that recommends the very best feature length journalism. We asked the editors to pick their all-time favourite articles, and this is what they chose:

[The Rise and Fall of Silk Road by Joshua Bearman and Tomer Hanuka](https://href.li/?https://www.wired.com/2015/04/silk-road-1/) - How a 29-year old idealist built a global drug bazaar and became a murderous kingpin.

[Failure and Rescue by Atul Gawande](https://href.li/?http://www.newyorker.com/news/news-desk/failure-and-rescue) - In commencement addresses like this, people admonish us: take risks; be willing to fail. But this has always puzzled me.

[The Sea of Crises by Brian Phillips](https://href.li/?https://grantland.com/features/sumo-wrestling-tokyo-japan-hakuho-yukio-mishima-novelist-seppuku/) - On sumo, ritual suicide and the death of samurai culture

[The High Is Always the Pain and the Pain Is Always the High by Jay Kang](https://href.li/?https://themorningnews.org/article/the-high-is-always-the-pain-and-the-pain-is-always-the-high) - Gambling addiction is a simple disease. Living the addiction is a bit more complicated.  

[Blowing Up by Malcolm Gladwell](https://href.li/?https://cpb-us-e2.wpmucdn.com/faculty.sites.uci.edu/dist/2/51/files/2018/03/NYorker2002-blowingup.pdf) - How Nassim Taleb turned the inevitability of disaster into an investment strategy.

[The Falling Man by Tom Junod](https://href.li/?https://www.esquire.com/news-politics/a48031/the-falling-man-tom-junod/) - An unforgettable story

[The Case for Reparations by Ta-Nehisi Coates](https://href.li/?http://www.theatlantic.com/magazine/archive/2014/06/the-case-for-reparations/361631/) - Two hundred fifty years of slavery. Ninety years of Jim Crow. Sixty years of separate but equal. Thirty-five years of racist housing policy. Until we reckon with our compounding moral debts, America will never be whole.

[Trial by Fire by David Grann](https://href.li/?https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2009/09/07/trial-by-fire) - Did Texas execute an innocent man?

[Frank Sinatra Has a Cold by Gay Talese](https://href.li/?http://www.esquire.com/news-politics/a638/frank-sinatra-has-a-cold-gay-talese/) - The defining piece of journalism about Frank Sinatra, and one of the most celebrated magazine stories ever published.

[This is Water by David Foster Wallace](https://href.li/?http://web.archive.org/web/20080213082423/http://www.marginalia.org/dfw_kenyon_commencement.html) - A stark but hopeful perspective on life’s fundamental questions.
