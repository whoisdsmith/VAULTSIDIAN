## About CourtListener's RECAP Archive

### Background

The RECAP Archive is a searchable collection of millions of PACER documents and dockets that were gathered using our [RECAP Extensions for Firefox, Chrome and Safari](https://free.law/recap/). PACER is the government-run system where nearly every legal document is filed from federal cases. The archive also includes [every free PACER opinion](https://free.law/2017/08/15/we-have-all-free-pacer/). This unique archive was created to partially address what we see as the [PACER Problem](https://free.law/2015/03/20/what-is-the-pacer-problem/).

Using this archive, you can finally search and download millions of PACER documents, including those that were originally scanned PDFs, [which we laboriously convert to text](https://free.law/2016/09/26/extracting-text-millions-of-pacer-documents/).

This work was made possible thanks to a partnership with [The Center for Information Technology Policy at Princeton](https://citp.princeton.edu/), the generous data contributions of thousands of RECAP users, and a ton of [volunteer support](https://free.law/thanks/).

If you find this work valuable, or if it saves you money on your PACER bill, please donate.
